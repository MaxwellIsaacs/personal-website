Complete Transparency

All interactions are public and auditable. Every view, upvote, comment, and even private message is visible to all network participants. Immutable posts cannot be deleted or modified.

Fully Decentralized

P2P networking using libp2p with automatic peer discovery via mDNS. No central servers or authorities—each node is equal in the network.

Cryptographically Secured

Ed25519 digital signatures for all content and interactions. Anonymous identities require no personal information, yet all data integrity is cryptographically verifiable.

Public Audit Trail

Every interaction with content is logged and publicly available. View counts, engagement metrics, and user activity are completely transparent with real-time verification.

No Content Moderation

Decentralized nature means no central authority can censor content. The platform explores what happens when transparency and free speech are taken to their logical extreme.

Zero Dependencies

Built with modern Rust networking stack including libp2p, sled database, and tokio async runtime. Self-contained with minimal external dependencies.

System Architecture

Storage & Types Layer
Local sled database, cryptographic identity management, immutable content structures

Core Philosophy

This platform challenges the fundamental assumptions of modern social media through radical transparency and decentralization.

  • No user accounts or registration required
  • Anonymous cryptographic identities
  • Complete transparency of all interactions
  • Immutable content and public audit trails

Technical Implementation

Built with Rust's modern networking and cryptography ecosystem for maximum performance and security.

  • libp2p for peer-to-peer networking
  • Ed25519 digital signatures
  • Sled embedded database
  • Tokio async runtime

Educational Project Disclaimer

This is a thought experiment and educational project. The complete lack of privacy and content moderation makes this unsuitable for real-world social networking. It's designed to explore the technical possibilities of radical transparency in decentralized systems and understand the implications of such an approach.
